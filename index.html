Overview of Volleyball

Volleyball is a dynamic team sport that combines athleticism, strategy, and teamwork. Played by two teams of six players each, separated by a high net, the objective is to score points by grounding the ball on the opponent's court while preventing them from doing the same.

Originating in the United States in 1895, volleyball has evolved into a global phenomenon with millions of participants worldwide. The sport demands agility, strength, coordination, and quick decision-making, making it both challenging and exhilarating.

Whether played recreationally in backyards or professionally in international arenas, volleyball offers endless opportunities for competition and camaraderie.

Famous Players

Volleyball has produced many legendary athletes who have shaped the sport's history:

  • Karch Kiraly - Three-time Olympic gold medalist, pioneer of beach volleyball
  • Giba (Gilberto Amauri) - Brazilian superstar with multiple Olympic medals
  • Logan Tom - American setter known for exceptional skills
  • Kim Yeon-koung - South Korean player with numerous international achievements
  • Lauren Gibbemeyer - American middle blocker with Olympic success

Key Facts

  • Players: 2 teams of 6 players each
  • Court Dimensions: 18m x 9m
  • Net Height: 2.43m (men), 2.24m (women)
  • Ball Weight: 260-280g
  • Olympic Sport: Since 1964
  • Global Reach: Played in over 200 countries
